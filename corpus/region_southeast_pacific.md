# Region: Southeast Pacific / Chilean Coast

## Coverage
45-20°S latitude, 85-70°W longitude. Includes the Humboldt Current system and Chilean Patagonian fjords.

## Why it matters for Nereus
The 2016 Chile MHW-HAB event is the single most economically documented marine heatwave in the global record — 27 million farmed salmon killed, ~$800M in losses. It is the canonical case for pitching Nereus to aquaculture operators.

## Economic profile
Chile is the world's second-largest producer of farmed Atlantic salmon, accounting for approximately 25% of global supply. Farms are concentrated in the Los Lagos, Aysén, and Magallanes regions of Patagonia.

## Risk profile
Chilean salmon farms are open-pen systems in semi-enclosed fjords, making them acutely vulnerable to HAB outbreaks triggered by warm-water intrusion. Typical El Niño events now carry documented aquaculture loss risk.

## Key vulnerability indicator
Concurrent positive ONI and positive PDO is the climate-mode signature that historically triggered the 2016 event. Nereus monitors this combination as a priority alert pattern.
