# Nereus user: aquaculture operations manager

## Profile
Runs salmon farms in Chile or Norway, oyster beds in the Pacific Northwest, shrimp ponds in Southeast Asia. Typically manages $20M-$100M in live biomass at any given time. Does not have in-house oceanographic expertise.

## Pain
Loses stock when SSTs spike above species-specific thresholds. The 2016 Chile event killed 27 million farmed salmon in a single event. Current warning systems are designed for hurricanes and extreme weather, not sub-surface thermal events.

## What Nereus gives them
A 14-day forward-looking probability of marine heatwave onset at their exact farm coordinates, delivered as a spoken or text-based briefing in their operating language. Alerts trigger decisive action: adjust feeding rates, partial harvest, insurance notification, net-pen relocation.

## Why it's valuable
A single successful early warning can prevent tens of millions of dollars in losses. Willingness-to-pay for a validated MHW forecast is estimated at $5K-$25K per farm per year.

## Market size
Global aquaculture is a $300B industry with $8B per year in documented MHW-attributable losses. The salmon farming sub-sector alone is $20B globally.
