# Species impact during the North Atlantic 2023 heatwave

## Summary
The 2023 event was the most intense North Atlantic MHW on record (+4.5°C peak).

## Key observations
Florida Keys coral bleaching, Atlantic hurricane-season fuel, Gulf Stream meander anomalies. iNaturalist captured 334 distinct species.

## Coverage
Heat content anomalies reached deeper than any prior Atlantic event, with implications for AMOC stability research.

## Biological lesson
Even the thermally stable North Atlantic is vulnerable to extreme events under reduced aerosol cooling and slowing circulation.

## Data source
iNaturalist research-grade observations, accessed via iNaturalist API v1, filtered to marine iconic taxa (Actinopterygii, Mollusca, Mammalia, Aves) within the canonical event bounding box. Each event window is paired with a matching baseline window from a climatologically similar non-event year for anomaly computation.
