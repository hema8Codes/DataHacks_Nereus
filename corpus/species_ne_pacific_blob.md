# Species impact during the NE Pacific Blob (2013-2015)

## Summary
iNaturalist research-grade observations show dramatic species shifts during the Blob event.

## Key observations
Humpback whale observations jumped from 14 records in 2010 baseline to 68 in 2015 (4.9× increase), linked to anchovy prey concentration events documented by Santora et al. 2020. Pink salmon (Oncorhynchus gorbuscha) appeared in records where it was previously absent. Bald eagle sightings nearly tripled (14 → 38).

## Coverage
131 species appeared in the Blob window that were absent from the 2010 baseline; 45 species present in 2010 were absent during the Blob.

## Biological lesson
Warm-water species shift northward; cold-water specialists retreat. The ecosystem reorganization was documented in real time through citizen-science records.

## Data source
iNaturalist research-grade observations, accessed via iNaturalist API v1, filtered to marine iconic taxa (Actinopterygii, Mollusca, Mammalia, Aves) within the canonical event bounding box. Each event window is paired with a matching baseline window from a climatologically similar non-event year for anomaly computation.
