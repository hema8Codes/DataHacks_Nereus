# The subsurface signature of marine heatwaves

## Core physical insight
Marine heatwaves are not purely surface phenomena. In the Northeast Pacific during the 2013-2015 "Blob," Argo floats documented:

- Surface temperature +0.93°C above baseline (compared to 2010)
- Mixed-layer depth 8.9 meters shallower than baseline
- Integrated heat content 0-300 m elevated by approximately +10°C·m

## Why this matters
A shallow mixed layer is the physical mechanism that sustains surface anomalies. Normally, winter storms mix the upper ocean and dissipate accumulated summer heat. When the mixed layer is shallow, that dissipation pathway is blocked — the anomaly persists through winter and re-emerges the following summer.

## Forecasting implication
Mixed-layer depth is a leading indicator for MHW persistence. Subsurface Argo observations provide lead time that surface-only satellite SST cannot. Nereus's forecast model is built around this insight.

## Data source
Scripps EasyOneArgo data product (Wong et al. 2026), accessed via the Ifremer ERDDAP gateway. Features derived per profile include: mixed-layer depth (0.2°C from 10 m reference), thermocline depth (depth of max dT/dz between 20-300 m), stratification index, and integrated heat content.
