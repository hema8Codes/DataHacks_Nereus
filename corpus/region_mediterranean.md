# Region: Mediterranean Basin

## Coverage
30-45°N latitude, -5 to 40°E longitude. Includes the entire Mediterranean Sea.

## Why it matters for Nereus
The 2022 Mediterranean event was the most intense on instrumental record for the basin (+4.3°C peak), and the 2003 event is still considered a canonical reference for ecosystem impact. The basin is semi-enclosed, which amplifies MHW intensity because heat cannot be efficiently exported to deeper water.

## Unique characteristics
MHW events here affect deeper water than in open oceans — documented mortality at 40 m depth during 2003 and 2022. This makes coastal benthic communities (corals, sponges, seagrass) especially vulnerable.

## Ecological stakes
Posidonia oceanica seagrass meadows, Paramuricea corals, sponges, Mediterranean bivalves. Large-scale coral bleaching-equivalent events are ongoing.
