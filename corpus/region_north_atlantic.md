# Region: North Atlantic

## Coverage
40-65°N latitude, -60 to -10°W longitude. Includes the Gulf of Maine, Grand Banks, and eastern North Atlantic.

## Why it matters for Nereus
The 2023 North Atlantic event was the most intense on record for this region (+4.5°C peak). The Gulf of Maine subregion is one of the fastest-warming marine ecosystems globally.

## Key subregional features
- Gulf of Maine: multi-year warming regime punctuated by discrete MHWs; cod fishery collapse attributed in large part to this warming.
- Grand Banks: historically cold-water rich fishing grounds, currently undergoing rapid regime shift.
- Florida Keys (southern extension): coral bleaching vulnerability, especially during 2023.

## Driver research frontier
The 2023 event prompted research into aerosol forcing (reduced Saharan dust) and AMOC slowdown as contributing factors. This is an active area of climate science.
