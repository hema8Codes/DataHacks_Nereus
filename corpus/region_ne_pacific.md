# Region: Northeast Pacific Subarctic

## Coverage
35-55°N latitude, 125-160°W longitude. Includes the Gulf of Alaska core and the California Current upwelling system.

## Why it matters for Nereus
This region has hosted two of the most intense Severe-category marine heatwaves in the global record: the 2013-2015 "Blob" and the 2019-2020 event sometimes called "Blob 2.0." Both were driven by the same physical mechanism — a shallow mixed layer trapping surface heat under reduced mixing.

## Live observing infrastructure
Scripps operates the CCE1 mooring at approximately 35°N, 122°W (220 km offshore Point Conception). This mooring provides temperature and salinity profiles at 13 standard depths down to ~4000 m, updated near-daily. CCE1 serves as Nereus's real-time validation pin for the California Current.

## Argo coverage
Approximately 3,000-4,000 profiles per year in this region, depending on float density. Sufficient for high-confidence regional mean state reconstructions.

## Primary economic stakes
Pacific salmon (multiple species), Pacific sardine, Pacific hake, market squid, Dungeness crab. Annual commercial value for the US West Coast alone is approximately $600M.
