# Species impact during the Chile 2016 MHW-HAB event

## Summary
The Chile 2016 event is best known for its effect on aquaculture rather than wild biodiversity.

## Key observations
27 million farmed Atlantic salmon (Salmo salar) killed by a Pseudochattonella harmful algal bloom triggered by the heatwave. Economic loss approximately $800 million (León-Muñoz et al. 2018).

## Coverage
Approximately 390 distinct species recorded in iNaturalist for the region in 2016 vs. 250 in 2013 baseline, reflecting observer effort more than biological change.

## Biological lesson
Aquaculture is the single most at-risk marine activity during MHWs, particularly where open-pen farming coincides with warm-water HAB-forming species.

## Data source
iNaturalist research-grade observations, accessed via iNaturalist API v1, filtered to marine iconic taxa (Actinopterygii, Mollusca, Mammalia, Aves) within the canonical event bounding box. Each event window is paired with a matching baseline window from a climatologically similar non-event year for anomaly computation.
