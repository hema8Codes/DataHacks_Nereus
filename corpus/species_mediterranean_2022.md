# Species impact during the Mediterranean 2022 heatwave

## Summary
The 2022 Mediterranean event was the hottest on instrumental record for the basin.

## Key observations
Mass mortality documented for red gorgonian corals (Paramuricea clavata), Posidonia oceanica seagrass, sponges, and bivalves. iNaturalist showed 461 distinct species in the event window.

## Coverage
Mortality extended to depths of 40+ meters — unusual for MHWs, which usually affect only the surface layer.

## Biological lesson
Semi-enclosed seas amplify MHW impacts because heat cannot export to deeper water masses.

## Data source
iNaturalist research-grade observations, accessed via iNaturalist API v1, filtered to marine iconic taxa (Actinopterygii, Mollusca, Mammalia, Aves) within the canonical event bounding box. Each event window is paired with a matching baseline window from a climatologically similar non-event year for anomaly computation.
